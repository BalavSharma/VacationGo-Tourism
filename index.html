Top Destination
Thimphu
Capital City Festivals Culture

Thimphu is Bhutan’s lively capital city, set in a scenic Himalayan valley. It blends modern life with deep tradition and is home to iconic sites, vibrant festivals, and rich cultural experiences.

View Detail
Must Visit
Paro
Airport Cliffside Heritage

Paro Valley is Bhutan’s gateway, home to the country’s only international airport and surrounded by farms, temples, dramatic peaks, cliffside Monastery, and vibrant annual festival.

View Detail
Best Experience
Punakha
Capital Valley Festivals

Punakha, Bhutan’s ancient capital, sits in a warm valley at 1,200 m. It is known for its majestic riverside dzong, blooming jacaranda trees, sacred festivals, and gentle scenic landscapes.

View Detail
Spiritual Heartland
Bumthang
Spiritual Temples Festivals

Bumthang is the spiritual core of Bhutan, filled with sacred temples, ancient fortresses, rolling valleys, and iconic festivals that preserve the kingdom’s deep Buddhist heritage.

View Detail
Must Visit
Tashichho Dzong & Tshechu
Fortress Festival Culture

Tashichho Dzong is a grand 17th-century fortress that houses government offices and sacred temples. Each autumn it hosts the Thimphu Tshechu, a 4-day celebration featuring masked Cham dances and colourful rituals that fill the dzong’s historic courtyard.

Highlights
Great Buddha Dordenma
Statue Viewpoint Spiritual

The Great Buddha Dordenma is a towering 54-meter gilded statue overlooking Thimphu Valley. It enshrines 125,000 smaller Buddha figures and offers sweeping views, making it one of Bhutan’s most significant spiritual landmarks.

Best Experience
Local Culture
Market Crafts Tradition

Thimphu’s cultural life thrives through its weekend farmers’ market, handicraft workshops, arts institutions, and archery grounds. Its vibrant streets and artisan spaces show how modern Bhutan blends seamlessly with centuries-old traditions.

Must Visit
Tiger’s Nest
Cliffside Sacred Iconic

Tiger’s Nest is an 8th-century monastery built around Guru Rinpoche’s sacred cave. Clinging to a cliff 900 m above the Paro Valley, it is one of Bhutan’s most breathtaking pilgrimage sites and a defining symbol of the country’s spiritual heritage.

Highlights
Rinpung Dzong
Fortress Monastery Heritage

Rinpung Dzong is a 17th-century fortress-monastery that overlooks Paro Valley. It houses monks and district offices, and its grand architecture reflects Bhutan’s deep cultural and administrative traditions.

Best Experience
Paro Tshechu
Festival Rituals Culture

The Paro Tshechu is one of Bhutan’s largest festivals, bringing villagers and visitors together each year. Sacred mask dances and vibrant rituals celebrate Bhutanese spirituality and community life.

Explore Now
Paro International Airport
Gateway Scenic Unique

Paro International Airport is Bhutan’s sole international airport, set among steep mountains and terraced fields. It serves as the dramatic gateway to the country’s valleys, temples, and landscapes.

Top Destination
Punakha Dzong
Fortress Rivers Historic

Built in 1637–38, Punakha Dzong stands at the meeting of the Po and Mo rivers. Known as one of Bhutan’s most beautiful dzongs, it showcases grand architecture framed by blooming jacaranda trees in spring.

Must Visit
Punakha Festivals
Tshechu Drubchen Rituals

Held in February or March, the Punakha Tshechu and Drubchen feature masked dances, sacred rituals, and the dramatic unfurling of the giant Thongdrel, making them among Bhutan’s most vibrant celebrations.

Highlights
Winter Capital
Mild Scenic Historic

Punakha serves as Bhutan’s winter capital due to its mild climate. The king and chief abbot spend the season here, reflecting the valley’s cultural and political significance.

Best Experience
Scenic Walks
Trails Fields Temples

Punakha offers riverside hikes, rice-field paths, a long suspension bridge over the Po Chu, and visits to Chimi Lhakhang, the famed “Divine Madman” fertility temple.

Must Visit
Jambay Lhakhang
Ancient Sacred Historic

Jambay Lhakhang is a 7th-century temple and one of Bhutan’s oldest sacred sites. It hosts the annual Jambay Drup festival, famous for its spiritual dances and fire blessing rituals.

Highlights
Jakar Dzong
Hilltop Fortress Iconic

Built in 1549, Jakar Dzong sits high above Jakar town. Its vast structure symbolizes Bumthang’s religious heritage and offers sweeping views across the valley.

Top Destination
Autumn Festivals
Tshechu Dance Ritual

Bumthang’s autumn festivals, including Jakar Tshechu and Jambay Lhakhang Drup, showcase dramatic Cham dances, fire blessings, and traditional performances cherished by locals and travellers.

Best Experience
Rural Culture
Cheese Crafts Meadows

Bumthang’s rolling valleys offer artisanal experiences—cheesemaking, weaving, chocolate production, and peaceful meadows that highlight the region’s rich rural heritage.
